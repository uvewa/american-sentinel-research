# Uncertainties — American Sentinel, September 10, 1891, Vol. 6, No. 36

## CRITICAL NOTE ON SCAN QUALITY

This issue presented exceptional difficulty due to very small type size in the scanned images, combined with moderate scan quality. Multiple articles spanning pages 283-286 were particularly affected. Many passages required extensive interpretation and remain uncertain. A higher-resolution scan is strongly recommended for verification of this entire issue.

---

## Page 281, Column 2, Article "The Prohibition Party and National Reform"

### Paragraph 2 — Theocracy passage
- **Second pass completed:** Yes
- **Transcribed as:** 'There is a true theocracy (God himself is the Ruler; in this, man is of God, but not by his authority, just as popes and kings ruled in the Dark Ages, "Drunken with the blood of saints and with the blood of the martyrs.")'
- **Confidence:** LOW — The parenthetical construction is unclear and the text within it is very difficult to parse
