> Graysville, Tenn., July 15, 1891.
>
> EDITOR AMERICAN SENTINEL: There is in a valley about the Cove, three or four miles from Dayton, a small company of Sabbath keepers, who are very conscientious and devout Christians. These not long ago were wicked and profane men. For many years, they with their neighbors and friends had indulged in all kinds of work and amusement on Sunday, but recently several of them accepted the observance of the Seventh-day Sabbath, being either Adventists or Seventh-day Baptists. Their friends now became their foes. So long as they all rested on Sunday, they said nothing at all about Sunday desecration; no matter how much was done on that day, it was all right. And that has been the custom in that vicinity for many years.
>
> Last week the Deputy Sheriff and two other men were over from Cove to make arrests. One Sabbath keeper keeps at work; and some of their nearest neighbors have been keeping exercise on Sunday; and this creates or is a thicket to catch their innocent people. It is almost inconceivable that such a spirit can still survive anywhere in this country, but that it does is evident. It is to be hoped that the Seventh-day Christians of southeastern Tennessee will boldly take up the gauntlet thus thrown down by bigotry and intolerance and bravely do battle for liberty of conscience as it has been done in the western part of the State.
