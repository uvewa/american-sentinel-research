# Uncertainties — American Sentinel, December 24, 1891 (v06n50)

## CRITICAL NOTE ON THIS ISSUE

This issue was transcribed from scanned newspaper images (PNG files) at a resolution that made much of the small newspaper text extremely difficult to read with confidence. The overall quality of the scans for this issue is notably lower than typical, with significant blurring and loss of detail in many passages. As a result, this issue contains an unusually high number of uncertainties and likely errors compared to IDML-sourced transcriptions.

**Recommendation:** This entire issue should be verified against higher-resolution scans or original documents before being considered publication-ready. Many passages below are flagged, but there may be additional errors throughout that were not caught.

## General Notes

1. **Text flow between columns/pages:** The American Sentinel uses standard newspaper column flow (top to bottom, then next column left to right). However, in this issue, some articles span multiple pages and the exact transition points are sometimes ambiguous, particularly for "Whence Came It All?" which spans pages 393-394.

2. **Article boundaries:** Some article boundaries are unclear, particularly on pages 395-397 where text may have been incorrectly attributed between "Shall Sabbath Observance Be Enforced by Law?", "Religious Ballot Box Stuffing", and the "Church and State" article.

3. **Scan quality:** The PNG scans appear to be from microfilm or early digital scans. Many characters are fuzzy, especially in the smaller typefaces used for body text. Headlines and larger type are more reliably readable.
