## Philadelphia and Chicago Sundays.

"WHAT has" what "fools these mortals" be! At the Sunday closing of the World's Fair, and exposing some well defined views in the following trenchant paragraphs:

The World's Fair is likely to be the pride of nothing more, for all, what is known as the Sunday Question. We do, we in the least care for the directors or commissioners who are now under so much pressure, for we shall be at the Fair open on Sundays. The ultimate decision will rest upon a higher, whatever the officials of the Fair may say to decide; and it needs only such an occasion as a great and widely attended public exhibition to bring the case before the people and make the issue so present the manifest evidence which they need, to pass judgment upon the question.
