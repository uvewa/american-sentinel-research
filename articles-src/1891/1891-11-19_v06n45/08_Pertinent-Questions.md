## Pertinent Questions.

THE senior editor, *Herald,* Battle Creek, Michigan, published the following series of questions, addressed to Rev. G. DeLa Mater, in relation to his advocacy; and it has been largely distributed in Michigan, and elsewhere:

1. Is there not Sunday work done on a Sunday morning newspaper than on a Monday morning paper?

2. Are not the large Sunday editions really the most demoralizing of all newspaper publications?

3. Is there not Sunday work by anybody on Sunday in this town besides a few barbers?

4. Cannot all refuse to read it who choose to do so?

5. Has a minister or any one else a right to take one man's livelihood to compel them to obey or to comply with what he conceived proper?

6. Is there anything moral and constitutional and ethical in these about the Sunday?

8. Is not the only way to make them truly Christian to get Christ into the people?

9. Can not to make Christianity live in the community by law is the best for each individual to be practicing conversion or by kindness?

10. Is there not a law on the books all ready for the purpose; let them keep so at words they keep at the soul. If the law be enacted to punish then those who be, who will escape persecution? None but those who are, who gave way against them, unless it is not of their belief to close their Sunday on at once. We must therefore your theory and practice in actual test.

11. When, did Christ, an expressed, say this to us when we all were a duty? What would you have us to do, every or every Friday night? What if they did not seek to run their Sunday in to actual rest?

In a post "Sabbath" for Mr. Jones, 1889, the Seventh-day Adventist community of Battle Creek it is left admirably free to observe the Sabbath as well as the close in this day, so was others in the matter of work. Cancellation of any Sunday statutes is time to be one who is ever faithful and prosperous; I observe the Sabbath and would not to be a Seventh day Adventist, it is against the interests of the Government, if this condition should be reached where in the limit of "religious test" its denomination affairs may not be applied.

W. M.
