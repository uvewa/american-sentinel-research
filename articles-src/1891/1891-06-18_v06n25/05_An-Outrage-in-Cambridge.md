This is very properly characterized as an outrage. The Constitution of Massachusetts provides that "every subject has the right to be secure from all unreasonable searches and seizures of his person, his houses, his papers, and all his possessions," and that all, and all, search must be upon warrant "supported by oath or affirmation" setting forth the grounds of the search and the persons or places to be searched. Therefore this wholesale and reckless search of a funeral procession was in direct violation of the Constitution of Massachusetts, or in the law of the Constitution.
