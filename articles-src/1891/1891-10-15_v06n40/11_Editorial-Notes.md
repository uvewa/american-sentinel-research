NOTHING but the grace of God will keep men from sin, no matter what the course they may pursue. He, and that grace is not imparted by legislative enactments, nor by fear of the stake. It is the goodness of God that leads men to repentance, and wherever he has commanded either his ministers or the State to drive men to repentance by burning or stoning, or even by fines and imprisonment.

---

We cannot conceive of a church without doctrine. And religion cannot be introduced into the public schools unless it is doctrinal in the sense of being definite, positive, and precise. To speak of the Church without doctrine is to talk of daylight without the sun, of an effect without a cause.—*Rev. Dr. Strong.*

---

THE most Ecumenical Methodist Conference is now in session in Washington, D. C. to the 7th inst., and will continue its sessions until the 26th. It will be interesting to see the religio-political opinions now before the nations of the world.

---

The Christian Author thinks, or says, at least, that he misrepresent National Reformers and their methods. That our contemporary thinks there is not strange, as a Shakespeare puts it—

---

PRIVATE the State can teach religion it must have a religion to teach. But the State is not a single individual but is an aggregation of individuals, it can have a religion only in the sense of having a creed which it fosters.

---

MRS. CRAFTS has never discovered that famous sentiment concerning Sunday: "Take the religious coat and you take the truth," yet he has the temerity to assert that he is laboring only for a civil institution.
