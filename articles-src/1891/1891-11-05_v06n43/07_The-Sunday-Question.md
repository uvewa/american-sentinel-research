## The Sunday Question.

IN questions of public controversy, our opinion should be heard. Our republicanism and range of individual liberty comports with the well-being of society. Recent religious opinions are as widely diversified as the physical, intellectual, and moral constitutions of men are different, hence every man and woman, should be persuaded by his, her, and find of its equipment and practice of his opinions, provided that any one do not interfere with the rights of others.

The account of some strenuous work recently done on our streets in Atlanta, Georgia, during Sunday, the dirge, in the pulpit, and through the Saturday and other daily papers, made a bitter and unwarranted attack on those who had investigated Sunday work. This attack revealed the extremes in our midst, of a class of narrow-minded bigots who speak and act as though governments were instituted, and laws enacted, for their own exclusiveness and benefit.
