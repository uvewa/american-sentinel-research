The Treasurer of the National Religious Liberty Association has recently received the following communication from a gentleman who, though busily engaged in the manufacture of brick and tile, not only finds time, but has an inclination to enlighten his Christian brethren on the important subject of civil and religious liberty and the dangers of religious legislation. The letter reads as follows:—

Mr. W. H. EDWARDS—*Dear Sir:* Last December, as the result of a conversation with a member of the National Religious Liberty Association, for which I have ever since maintained the deepest respect and sympathy for your organization. Therefore I hope you will excuse me with last mail matter for distribution. I shall try to do as good a work in the Presbyterian Church of which I am a member.
