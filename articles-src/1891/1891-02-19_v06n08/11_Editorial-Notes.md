A BILL has been introduced into the California Legislature, providing that every establishment shall keep open any warehouses, or manufacturing, or any commercial establishment, other than a drug store, or any liquor or any report or Sunday between sunrise and sunset, shall be fined not more than five dollars, nor less than two dollars. The bill was referred to the Committee on Judiciary.

---

RAVANNA, this State, is moving in the direction of more direct enforcement of the Sunday law. On the 11th a Reading-room was formed at the annual of the Young Men's Christian Association with the pastors of the different churches. Its stated the leaders in the movement are and those who insist not just on Sunday, and who have the least of it, but those who are the most in compelling people to do whether they want to or not. There is a great deal of humming about the whole Sunday rest business.

---

A SUNDAY bill has been introduced in the California Legislature, at the request of the Woman's Christian Temperance Union of that State. Its technical description is "an act to amend an act entitled 'an act to establish a penal code,' approved February 14, 1872, by [64] adding to chapter VII, title IX, part 1, thereof, three new sections numbered respectively 799, 800, and 801, forbidding the publication, opening, or maintenance of a ball, beer, rack or prize fight, horse racing, or playing gambling house, or saloon, or any barbecue, or noisy amusement, or the keeping, conducting, or exhibiting of any theatre, or other place of sound, dramatic or operatic performance, where musical, the keeping open, on that day, any saloon or barber shop, or any butcher shop or other place of business for business purposes."

The Bill has been introduced by request, and it is understood that the gentleman who introduced it will not vote for it. It is now in the hands of the Committee on Education and Public Morals.

A RELIGIOUS paper published in a Western State, being grieved that a certain juvenile religious publication in the East had omitted any reference to Christianity.

There are base agents, and the natural tendency under false pretenses, and ready to assist in the various practices that result from being imposed upon, to prevent the latter from being imposed upon, and the purpose that is the right thing for these times to instill into their young and unsuspecting hearts.

Sure enough! why not? And in order to secure it effectually, why not pass a law and such religious instruction shall be given, why not make a law regulating the whole matter, saying what shall give the instruction and what they shall teach? It is what, to be sure, but what they call religion. In short, why not have a State religion in fact, and refuse to tolerate either error or dissent.
