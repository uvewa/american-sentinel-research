In the *American Sentinel* for June, Rev. James Brach, D.D., says that the "Christian Sabbath is in peril most of all from the sentimentalism of the Church. There is hardly any subject in the interest of which it is so hard to arouse more or to scatter an audience, even among Christians, on this subject of the Sabbath." He then follows with an exhortation to the ministers to agitate this question: "If the Christian conscience is aroused."

In another article in the same paper, Professor J. L. McIlhenny, Secretary of the fourth district of the Woman's Christian Temperance Union, in giving a report of his work in Wheeling, West Virginia, says: "I was told that it was a common thing for the grocery and the church members of a certain denomination do the same thing as the sabbath; and the pastor of the congregation had frequently spoken of these things." He further adds, that "the African Methodist Episcopal Church, Wheeling, had out a flaming placard for an excursion test Sabbath from Pittsburg to Wheeling, at $1.00 for the round trip. Ministers from Pittsburg, and their congregation, pastors and their congregations of neighboring towns, were all invited to the grand rally on that day. If the law, the boldest and most outrageous act was the creation of the Sabbath by the Church that I have ever known."

Mr. Little, of Dorchester, Massachusetts, in a paper read before the Congregational Council of the World, at London, says: "No institution of society, not the Church, more difficult and important than any other, was to be right in its creative in the Sabbath. Some of the greatest Christians to the other observance of the day were the thoughtless habits of Christian people."

A minister in Duluth, Minnesota, in discussing the Sunday question, said: "Not Protestantism the rum-shops are closed, mails are being sent over the land, manufacturers in action, men are working immorally, and we poor Christians are patronizing their employers."
