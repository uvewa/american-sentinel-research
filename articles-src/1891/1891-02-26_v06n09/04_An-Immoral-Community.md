CALAMETTA is held up by the Sunday-law advocates as a terrible example of an immoral community, because it has no Sunday law, yet no doubt it is as any worse, or even as bad, as some States that have the most rigid Sunday law. It is, we believe, quite a rigid Sunday law, act, according to the public press. Rev. W. T. Tolson, a Baptist preacher of Columbus, delivered a sermon in that city not long since, in which he attached the social organization of the city, and painted Christians in the blackest colors of immorality. He said the city was in league with Satan, and in its research gave many black and disgusting details of the social wickedness, while the

> The Columbia Club, arch of the elite of South Columbia, and association, was as [page 67] a conspicuous institution. Its brown was at 6639 Laurel St., N.W., Washington, D.C.

demanded the Young Men's Christian Association or being to league with the question of pictures of a "revolving commission of prostitution to be posted on her property." He attacked the city authorities along the line of showing that they evidently knew to trust, and spoke plainly of demagoguism, which, he said, resulted from the meddling of the race problem.

His whole discourse was in this style. He says he can produce proof of this statement, and some exciting episodes are ahead.

It seems from all this that even a Sunday law is regarded of the usual standard as of wholeness of whatever privates reveals.
