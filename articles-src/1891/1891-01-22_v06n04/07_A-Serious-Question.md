A GOOD deal has been said throughout this country in the past year upon the subject of compulsory religious education, even in the atmosphere of compulsory religious education.

Is a good many instances Germany has been cited as a model Government in this respect, but the Keepers of the Augustan lately showed a disposition to take heroic measures, to a certain extent, with the result in its dominions, declaring that a given system of "eternal cramming" with religion was "the laws of their minds the nations suffer from the overpressure of its numbers and if according to overloading everywhere, for the number of whom it is now more than the nation can bear, and who constitute a disturbing element in politics.

England also has a system of compulsory education, and in her dominions influence to the throats at the opening of the last Parliament, the Queen of Great Britain called the attention of that body to another evil which is found there. She said "Your attention will be called to the expediency of alleviating the burdens which the law of compulsory education has within recent years imposed upon the poorer portions of the community."

In view of these two official statements by the Governments of two of the most civilized Governments of earth, and of the two which have enforced the system of compulsory education, those in this country who are so seriously striving to suggest the adoption of a system here, ought to be able to see clearly enough to consider that question. Attention has to this state of things been called by Horace Williams who said that education without character instead of being a benefit either to the individual or to the State, is a detriment to both instead of its being to the safety of the State is dangerous to the State.
