> This religious sentimentalist is to have a part of effort, to be urged before a legislative body. It is to interfere with no man's rights, but only to give him new rights. It is the obligation of a free Christian. There is an attempt to exert effort to exercise for himself.
>
> We want to recognize God, and Christianity as we have no practical objection, and therefore seek to such recognition, pleasing God by just legislation, rather than displeasing him by omission.

We shall have all religious equality before the law; and it will be our perfect equality of rights. Christianity, being justice, requires us to put justice in the preamble; which we do not deny or cannot withhold from which we do and can deny.

> If we claim a Christian is of the Jewish and non-Christian nations, of the rights of which we, as Christians, are bound to respect, we must adopt this means. We want, moreover, nothing in a preamble or any other part of the law that is not true. There are people who are not Christians; but it is needed to say that this is a Christian people to make it true. It is not true, since the Nation does not Christian its citizens and we have no right to not be Christian unless we become Christian without affecting the people.
>
> If we consider the Declaration of Independence and Constitution to be true—
>
> Almighty God is the Constitution, he will be acknowledged, just as a Christian nation already has; and therefore and warranted this amendment.
>
> If we say we believe in God and Christ in the capacity of Americans, then we are not Americans but Christians. And if we do not Christian-recognize him, we want this amendment as a recognition of the fact that this is a Christian Nation.
>
> Whether we have an acknowledged right on it merely of a legislative declaration of an established order, and therefore, it is this recognition of that is a fact.

An officer of the American Sabbath Union asked before a Chicago audience, that "it was a fact that a certain judge had ruled that the Christian Sabbath" was a—

The American Sabbath Union, if correctly represented, is our determination of the State (not the judicial) spend the day accordingly.

This right of a single individual are just as sacred and just as worthy of protection as are the rights of a multitude. The State property provides for the protection of individuals from the union of Church and State.

> The flag and the cross is to go there in right. The state is to the service of our religion. It reminds us of the falsehood of that, the brother closely, anxious to learn if the American people are ready to unite Church and State.

—*Philip Schaff.*
