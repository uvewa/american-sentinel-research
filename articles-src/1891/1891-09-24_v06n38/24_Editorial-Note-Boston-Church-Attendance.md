A RECENT count made in Boston by a religious journal revealed the fact that "the morning and afternoon attendance at 102 churches was only 71,069, while nearly 400,000 took trains or boats out of town, or rode in the horse cars, on recreation bent." Noting this fact in its relation to the demand for the closing of the Chicago Fair, a New York daily asks: "Are not the convictions and the wishes of the great majority of the people entitled to consideration in this democratic country?"
