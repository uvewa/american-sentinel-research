In the House, February 14, Hon. Joseph Taylor, of Indiana, speaking of Indian schools, said it is the intention of the Government to be non-partisan and non-sectarian. They ought to be exclusively Government schools. I have no authority to make of my church, or the contrary, I desire every church which has labored for Indian education, to Christianize the Indians. But it was never the aim of the Federal Government to support or encourage sectarian schools. The Constitution itself is not silent on this subject, and at best, thirty-six States in the Union have incorporated into their Constitutions provisions against supporting sectarian schools at public expense, including the six new States recently admitted.

We think it was Abraham Lincoln who said, that, "you can fool all the people part of the time, and part of the people all the time, but you can not fool all the people all the time." It is true in the matter of Sunday legislation; thousands are beginning to see that the liberty promised them, by the Sunday-law agitation, would prove to be only slavery.
