# Uncertainties — American Sentinel, June 25, 1891 (v06n26)

## General Note

This issue presented exceptional difficulty for transcription due to the very small print size, the density of the text across 8 pages, and the overall scan resolution. Many passages required significant interpretation. The confidence ratings below reflect this challenge.

---

## Article 01: "Be Careful What You Call It"

### Page 201, Column 2-3
- **Second pass completed:** Yes
- **Transcribed as:** "By it seems from this that whether a thing is religious or not depends altogether upon what you call it. If you call it religion, then it is such..."
- **Confidence:** LOW — The transition from the block quote to the editorial commentary is difficult to read precisely. The phrase "it is so-uh" vs. "it is such" is unclear.
- **Reason:** Very small print; letter shapes suggest "such" but could be another word.

### Page 201, Column 3

- **Confidence:** LOW — This sentence appears to reference Jeremiah 13:23 ("Can the Ethiopian change his skin, or the leopard his spots?") but the exact wording in the original is very difficult to read.

## Overall Assessment

This issue of the American Sentinel presented the most challenging transcription conditions encountered to date. The scan resolution combined with the extremely small typeface (particularly in the three-column format on pages 201-205) means that many passages could not be verified to a high standard. A higher-resolution scan would be needed to resolve the numerous LOW-confidence readings documented above. The article structure and general arguments are captured correctly, but individual words and phrases throughout should be verified against a better source if available.
