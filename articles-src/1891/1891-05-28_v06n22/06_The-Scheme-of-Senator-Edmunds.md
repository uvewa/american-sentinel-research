## The Scheme of Senator Edmunds.

[page 172]

The resignation of Senator Edmunds has not silenced his efforts and by any means, and meanwhile, he says, he will exert his utmost personal efforts and use all his influence to bring about the establishment of a great national University at Washington.

It is an old hobby of his, and a select committee to report on the scheme was appointed through his efforts in the Senate. His main purpose, his own words, is to make it a great non-sectarian university, maintained at the expense of the Federal Government, a great non-sectarian university, so in which nobody should make plain the liberality of Protestants as compared with the narrowness of Papal power!

It may be very desirable and very important to make this manifestation; but it is not the business of the United States Government to make it. The United States Government is not a religious power, either Roman Catholic or Protestant, and it has nothing to do with religious recognition. The sovereignty herein lies in the people, and they are made up of Catholics and Protestants, Jews and pagans, and people of no religions at all. Every person is free to think as he pleases about religious matters, and the Constitution protects him in that freedom. Any Church can set up a university or an array of universities as it likes, and teach whatever religious dogmas it prefers; and the private those who are liberal and which narrow must be left to the people themselves. They can support those they like and neglect those whose instruction is offensive to them. It is a free field in which everybody has a chance. The Roman Catholics have a university. It is not the nation's affair. The Jews are as much as the Christians.

Accordingly, the Government can not enter into the magnification by throwing its power in favor of any religious party, without outrage to the fundamental principles of our republican system. It is not its function to teach any religion or to [page 172] open any religion, or to show Protestant "liberality," or to expose Papal "narrowness." Each of those religious families must do its own showing, opposing, and competing for itself.

Outside of all questions of religion, too, the scheme for a National University at Washington, founded and maintained by the Government, is impracticable and absurd. What principles of political economy, for instance, would it teach? Should it change them with the changes of political control of the Government? If Congress sets up the university, it must regulate its curriculum and its policy and theory of education. As the political complexion of Congress varies, those would have to vary also.

Moreover, there is no occasion, no need for such a university. What is desirable rather, is the development of the universities which we already have. Non-sectarian education is already provided by most of them, and they carry liberality to religion.
