## Reports From the Field.

[page 174]

H. F. PHELPS, of Brainerd, Minnesota, a worker in the National Religious Liberty Association, sends in the following from letters recently received in response to circulars and literature sent out.—

The following is from a lawyer: "Any layman, either Mister or national, tending to secure or abridge the right of any man, whether in business or school, ought to be opposed, and given no rest, until all legislation touching to any other than a political or religious faith may to. Make life bright."

Another, a minister, writes: "I will hasten to reply, and I will take one for myself. I am for it in all its votes. "I Agree;" or, to use your own words, keep at the good work."

Another writes: I agree with the principles of the Association. I will be a sad day for the country when the State shall dictate our religion to us.

A County Commissioner writes: "I will do all I can for the Association. I subscribe to every article of faith. Anything I can do, be assured, I am willing and ready."

Another says: "I am in sympathy with the all of the principles."

A postmaster writes: "We should consider that the rights of conscience are a great thing. I received your letter and leaflets. The neighbors took quite an interest in the matter, and wanted me to get petitions [signed]. All of your papers are grand."
