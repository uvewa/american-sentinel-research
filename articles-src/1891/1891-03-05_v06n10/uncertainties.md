# Uncertainties — American Sentinel, Volume 6, Number 10 (March 5, 1891)

## General Notes

The scan quality of this issue is moderate. Many words are legible but the small type size and scanning resolution make individual characters difficult to distinguish in many places. A significant number of readings throughout this transcription are best-effort interpretations based on context and partial letter shapes.

---

## Structural Uncertainties

### Page 73, Column Layout
- **Issue:** The relationship between the left column article and the "What Does the Bible Teach about the Sabbath?" article is uncertain. The left column appears to contain editorial material about the Columbus Sunday Association and biblical discussion of the Sabbath, while the right column has the Crafts/American Sabbath Union article. Whether these are one continuous article or two separate pieces is unclear from the layout.
- **Decision:** Treated as one combined article under the "What Does the Bible Teach about the Sabbath?" heading, as the content is thematically related.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM

### Page 73, Right Column Top
- **Issue:** Text beginning "whether or not Sunday is the Sabbath..." appears above the "What Does the Bible Teach about the Sabbath?" heading in the right column. This could be continuation from the left column (column wrap) or part of the right column article.
- **Decision:** Included as part of the main article, as it connects to the discussion about the American Sabbath Union.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM

### Pages 75-76, Article Boundaries
- **Issue:** The boundary between "California and the Sunday Law" (ending with W. A. B.) and the next article "Human Enforcement of the Law of God" (signed F. Y. Cortinas) is clear. However, whether "God and Cæsar" is a separate article title on page 76 or a subtitle within "Human Enforcement" is uncertain.
- **Decision:** Created "Human Enforcement of the Law of God" as a separate article with Cortinas attribution. Did not create a separate "God and Cæsar" file since the content appeared to be part of the same piece.
- **Confidence:** MEDIUM

### Pages 77-78, The Statesman / Herald of Gospel Liberty
- **Issue:** The exact point where "The Statesman" ends and "Herald of Gospel Liberty" begins on page 78 is difficult to determine. The left column of page 78 appears to contain text from both articles.
- **Decision:** Attributed text about statesmen and religious liberty principles to "The Statesman" and text about the Herald of Gospel Liberty newspaper to the article of that name.
- **Confidence:** LOW
