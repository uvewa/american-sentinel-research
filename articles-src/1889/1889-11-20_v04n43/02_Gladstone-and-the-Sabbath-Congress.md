Mr. Gladstone wrote a letter to the late so-called Sabbath Congress in Paris advocating Sunday observance and laws for its enforcement, but he evidently wants such statutes not for himself but for the common herd. This is shown by the fact that on the Sunday preceding the congress Mr. Gladstone, who was in Paris, after attending church in the morning, drove with a party of friends in the Bois-de-Boulogne, and in the evening visited the great exposition.
