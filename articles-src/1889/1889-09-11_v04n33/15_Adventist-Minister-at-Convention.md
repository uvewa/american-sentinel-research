A Seventh-day Adventist minister was unceremoniously sat upon by a convention held in Minneapolis recently, that discussed the Sundaylaw question. The Adventist offered a resolution which he wished passed instead of one which a committee reported. Dr. Crafts, a Presbyterian minister from New York, asked the denomination of the Adventist, and when informed, said that "A Seventh-day Adventist had no place in that convention except as a spectator or reporter." We had thought the time was past for running things in such a high-handed way, but it seems we were mistaken. No men nor set of men need ever think to enforce a moral or religious principle without submitting it to an intelligent discussion.
