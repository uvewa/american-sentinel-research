In the line of the policy of compelling children by law to go to school is the theory of making voting by qualified citizens compulsory. A new idea of this theory in a modified form comes from Kansas City. It is a municipal arrangement, levying a poll-tax, which will be discharged by the act of voting, but collected upon a failure to vote, and the money turned into the school fund.
