---
title: "Any person who cannot be religious without having a law made"
date: 1898-03-31
publication: "American Sentinel"
volume: 13
issue: 13
attribution: "editorial"

principles:
  - "The Sabbath Question"

applications:
  - "Voluntary vs Coerced Religion"
  - "Sabbath Observance and Enforcement"
---

ANY person who cannot be religious without having a law made to regulate the conduct of other people, cannot be religious when such a law is enacted. No person can be a true Sabbath-keeper until he is able to keep the Sabbath independently of all human law and of the conduct of all other persons.
