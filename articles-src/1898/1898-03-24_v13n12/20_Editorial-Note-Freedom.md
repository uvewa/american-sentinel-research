FREEDOM is reason's native air and man's inalienable right. No man can righteously sell, bequeath, or give away this original and necessary condition of the human soul; nor can any other man, or body of men, righteously come into possession of this primitive and inherent right of another. Years ago, in New England, a case to recover a fugitive slave was brought before Judge Harrington. To prove his claim, the slave-owner produced in court a bill of sale. The judge returned it, saying that it was not satisfactory. The Southerner's blood warmed and he indignantly asked what would be satisfactory to his honor.
