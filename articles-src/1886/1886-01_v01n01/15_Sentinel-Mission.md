There is in the land no paper as large as the Sentinel, published monthly, the contents of which is got up with so great care, which is offered at so small a price. The publishers will not make money in the enterprise, but they will have the satisfaction of knowing that they are doing good service to the cause of our country, and of our common humanity. We expect that all, without respect to party or beliefs, who love "justice, liberty, and equality," will give us their support, and aid in extending the circulation of the Sentinel. We intend that every article shall be carefully prepared, and nothing be admitted to its columns to which anyone can reasonably take exceptions.
