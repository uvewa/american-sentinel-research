"BISHOP POTTER," it is said, "aided and backed by many others, has opened a campaign against the bribe-taking of the New York City police, which is said to cover $5,000,000 a year." Certainly the bishop has undertaken a large task. If bribe taking should cease in New York, how would the political machines be supported? Why, the political "boss" would have to retire from business, not only in New York, but in a good many other cities also.
