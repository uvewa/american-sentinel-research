THE ready response of the whole country to the cry of stricken Galveston, and the exhibition of the spirit that refuses to be dismayed in the face of overwhelming ruin, but is determined to repair the disaster, are two things pleasant to contemplate in connection with the most appalling catastrophe known in American history.
