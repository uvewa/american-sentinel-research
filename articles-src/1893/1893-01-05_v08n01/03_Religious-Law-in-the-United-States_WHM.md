In this country civil interference with religion is confined to statutes, and legal precedents, enforcing the observance of Sunday; providing penalties against blasphemy and profanity; fixing the necessary qualifications of witnesses; invalidating Sunday contracts; disqualifying ministers of the gospel," and also one day of rest they keep "a Supreme Being or a state of faith in holding certain public offices; and requiring Bible reading in public schools. Of these none has as yet made any trouble or given any offense except the statutes requiring Sunday observance.

All such statutes and legal precedents are of foreign origin in our law. They are of foreign origin. They came from three different sources—Puritan, Cavalier, and Roman Catholic. The Puritan still say he still be treated. That Puritan made his own laws, his own rules and his own statutes; and, consequently, he looks to the sword to maintain the authority of those laws, rules and statutes. The Cavalier transferred his allegiance from one sovereign to another; accepted the body of law and legal precedents; and the authority of these he accepted with the same comity, and upon the same assumptions, timidly. The Roman Catholic is Supranational. He gives his allegiance to the Pope, and never lost the evidences of the Latin occupation.

Against these steadily flowing streams of precedent for the appeal of religious to civil authority is religion against the Constitution of the United States itself. And the language of the Constitution is against religious legislation, and of religious legislation alone. And clear as this language is, and until now it is true that scarcely a statute stands upon the United States statute books in relation to religion. That is because we report to the Federal Constitution is establishing the authority of the courts in every territory. And while the civil prohibition of common labor and business on Sunday, of liquor and the providing of, or attendance on, places of entertainment on Sunday, or by constitutional or other court, in locality, is constitutionally in all the States, has been supreme from the first. Such decisions exist, still the State courts decide, to this time, still now the body of religious legislation in this country was not accomplished by a steady overriding of the Constitution. That steady overriding has completely overwhelmed the constitution.
