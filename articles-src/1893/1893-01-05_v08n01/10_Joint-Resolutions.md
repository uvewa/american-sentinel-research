IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES

DECEMBER 12, 1892.

*By the Select Committee on the World's Columbian Exposition.*

JOINT RESOLUTION

*Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,* That the act entitled "An act to aid in carrying out the act of Congress approved April twenty-fifth, eighteen hundred and ninety-one, and to aid in carrying out the act of Congress approved August fifth, eighteen hundred and ninety-two, relating to the World's Columbian Exposition," approved August fifth, eighteen hundred and ninety-two, be, and the same is hereby amended by striking out, in section three, of said act, all after the word "thereof," in line nine, and by adding thereto the following: "And it is hereby declared that it is the intent of the act of Congress, approved August fifth, eighteen hundred and ninety-two, to prohibit the opening of said Exposition on the first day of the week, commonly called Sunday, and that the managers of said Exposition are hereby directed to enforce the provisions of this act; and it shall not be a condition of the appropriation hereby made or any previous appropriation, that the World's Columbian Exposition shall not be open on the first day of the week, and to shall be, and is hereby made, a part of the rules of said Exposition for no general observance of any day of the week as a Sabbath or day of worship; nor to make any rules or exhibitions of the rules of the Exposition in connection with any observance of any day of the week as a Sabbath or day of religious worship.
