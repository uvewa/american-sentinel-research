The present proposal for discussion of the question, "Ought the Sunday law of the State to be repealed or modified? is a shock to the sensibilities of many good people. It is established so that in one this custom is a thing so much difference differently from any other law, as to be the tradition to it in their education to relate to their religion. What men overlook that to repeal it "is an attack upon a sacred institution." To transgress they think not half so bad, but "to repeal it" to so much greater extent than transgression. . . . how much better it will be for men we hardly say, "I'm shocked."

The traditions and friends of this Sunday law, persisting at once in its relation to their religion, verifies my own thoughts, that it is a religious matter.
