AT one of the four mass-meetings held under the auspices of the Chicago Baptist Union in Chicago on the evening of February 5, Rev. Johnston Myers, of Columbus, Ohio, said:—

Disastrous events are possible if the nations will visit Chicago. They will come, of course, from every civilized country. And if they will not see the American Sabbath, and if the World's Fair is opened on Sunday, with Sunday opening and chicken, I am in favor of closing these doors.

In proposing a choice upon such matters for Almighty God to do, these Sunday law advocates are getting really to presume too much, and are claiming a great deal as on a sayings from the Almighty for Congress. For purely selfish which is easily predicted they would, in a work published in 1888, in the *National Perils*:

Satan works through the elements also to garner his harvest of unprepared souls. He has studied the secrets of the laboratories of nature, and he uses all his power to control the elements as far as God permits. . . . When he was suffered to afflict Job, how quickly flocks and herds, servants, houses, children, were swept away, one trouble succeeding close upon another as in a moment. It is God alone who protects men as a great physician who can heal and like also restore the feeble . . . And in proportion as his power is there are no safeguards, even now he is at work. In accidents and calamities by sea and by land, in great conflagrations, in fierce tornadoes, in terrific hail storms, tempests, floods, cyclones, tidal waves, and earthquakes, in every place and in a thousand forms Satan is exercising his power. He sweeps away the ripening harvest, and famine and distress follow. He imparts to the air a deadly taint, and thousands perish by the pestilence. These visitations are to become more and more frequent and disastrous. Destruction will be upon the beast of the earth. The beasts of the field will groan, and the earth will languish.

When of an old time great destruction was predicted, it was ever done in connection with a previous warning to the people. God has charged the Nation that the disaster will be upon us. They have charged then the charges will charge all their troubles upon the adherents of truth, they shall be hailed in its contempt into the . . . and that claimed that the Nation is offending God by the violation of a Sunday ordinance, and also that day observance shall be strictly enforced, and that ultimately it will be seen that there will be punishment, thus destroying reverence for Sunday. And he answered, I have not troubled Israel, but thou and thy father's house, in that ye have forsaken the commandments of the Lord, and thou hast followed Baalim, 1 Kings 17, 18. As the wrath of the people shall be excited against those who make conscience of the keeping of God's commandment in the Sabbath, which has Israel pursued toward Elijah.
