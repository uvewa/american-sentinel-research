About a month and a half since, Rev. Wilbur Crafts came to Bradford, and worked four or five days in the endeavors to bring up an activity on this line. Meetings were held in all of the churches. He organized a Sunday Rest-day League, the same as he has in regular occupation doing throughout the country. This league, with a good membership, included a president, a secretary and treasurer, and provided for appreciation of a secret committee. Yet the business and financial men, who are known to the general membership of the associations of their Christian churches and mission. Their duties were to note who were in the establishment on Saturday and who were witnesses thereto, and to report to the establishment; and then another other committee was to proceed with the prosecution of offenders or gratification for a might be deemed best. It is now about a year and a half since this registration was effected. Mr. Crafts' paper has been coming week by week to many of the ministers to supply inspiration; but to the present time, I have not learned that, with all of his expenditure of effort not a soul has been punished to make one prosecution in our cities.
