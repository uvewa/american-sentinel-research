THERE "mass-meetings" in Chicago, for the purpose of representing that public opinion in that city is in favor of the Sunday closing of the World's Fair, reminds us of the methods used in the annals of Great Britain, when three tailors, of Fosters' Market (London), met and adopted the high-sounding resolution, "Resolved, that we, the people of England..."
