EDITOR AMERICAN SENTINEL: The Lutheran Church, as a Protestant body in this country, considers its greatest mission to be, protest; and in so far as she does protest, she will continue to stand in the front of the Protestant churches of America, and will cherish, protect, and ennoble her traditions; and to that extent does she also wish to preserve intact the purity of the first principles of the Reformation. If this purpose can only be exercised in the free spirit of obedience to the Word of God, it can only do that functionally and efficiently. The idea of this Government forever separate the affairs of religion from the affairs of State, and Church and State as field coordinate is fundamental. If therefore it proves "childish," as most writers on the subject say, to look for any Sunday law—
