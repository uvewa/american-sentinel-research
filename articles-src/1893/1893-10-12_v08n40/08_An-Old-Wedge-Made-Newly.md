THE drive on the part of the Catholic Church for a diversion, and a share of public money for the support of its parochial schools are many and varied in their character. Nor is the endeavor made without loss of time, varying each approach, and presenting the case to each audience with an air of plausible reasoning. Fairness, and even generosity to all, is the way for the destruction of the public school system, and the diversion of the funds among church schools. In all her political and ecclesiastical methods the Catholic Church, and her hierarchy and priesthood, presented to be raked as one. We do not propose that the government at large shall pay for it.
