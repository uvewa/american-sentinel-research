An idea which obtained considerable currency in the past year among a certain class of religionists was to the effect that the Sunday closing of the World's Fair, as being the best evidence that the government of the United States, would be the best proof that could be given of the genuineness of "American Christianity;" and that this action would convince the world of its truth, or at least ought to do so, that "this is a Christian Nation."

Most of the readers of the SENTINEL, however, honestly disagree with any such notions; and it would be a good thing for the Nation, if every individual holding to that position could have been brought in question. I am glad to know, that, by attending the World's Congress, it has opened the eyes of thousands of our countrymen to see the light of truth in this matter.
