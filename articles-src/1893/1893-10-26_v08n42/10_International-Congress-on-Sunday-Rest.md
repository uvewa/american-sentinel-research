THE *Evangel and Sabbath Outlook,* in its issue of October 7, has a report of the "International Congress on Sunday Rest, at Chicago, September 28-30."

Its printed programme announced one hundred papers or addresses. The speakers came from all the principal countries of Europe, from Great Britain, from Canada. A few came from still more remote points: from Belgium, Hungary, sighters from France, emissors from Holland, speakers from Scotland and Switzerland, from Germany; the best known name, and about eighty from the United States and Canada. A few countries not named were represented by letters and documents addressed to the officers of the congress, two from Rome.

Speakers following the Man Patterson, said the congress opened. Among those who were present, the majority of were women who came from all parts of the world, and were mostly members of the labor or the working classes.
