It is refreshing to find a judge who can grasp the thought that a mere annoyance of somebody's religious prejudice is not a disturbance of the peace, and is not cognizable by civil courts.

Brink, in his "History of Civilization," defines the functions of civil government thus: "To maintain order, to prevent the strong from oppressing the weak, and to adopt certain precautions regarding the public health, and the like services which any government can render to the interests of civilization."

This evening World of this city, he makes that "Attorney-General Olney goes out of his way to aid and encourage the various sectarian trusts which seek to make Sunday a closed day in America. He would be much more fittingly and properly engaged in getting the way to block the more material trusts which rob labor for monopoly's enrichment."
