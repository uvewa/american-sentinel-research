THE reunion of religious congresses will open in Chicago, September 11, and continue seventeen days. The object of this Congress as set forth in the prospectus stated by J. H. Barrows, D.D.: "To furnish a great school of comparative religions; to bring the different faiths into contact and conference; to deepen the spirit of brotherhood; to bring out the distinctive truths of each religion; to show why and how each cause of the future life; to bridge the chasms between Christians of different names and cultivate goodness of men of all names; to induce men to work together for a common good; and to promote the cause of international peace."
