JUDGED BY THEIR OWN TERMS.

THE survey of the subject would not be complete without some reference to the savings of "necessity" and "charity," which are made in all Sunday laws.

The very presence of the word "charity" is sufficient to betray to the true nature of these laws as religious dogmas enacted into statutes. The interpretation of the word has, of course, to be made according to its context. And indeed it is correctly said that "the statute which long established and common usage of religious exemptions on the basis of necessity has been recognized, the course of religion may be deemed broad enough to be described as relating to the closets of the religious character of the law in question." But surely this admission that of "necessity" introduces a degree of uncertainty as to the application of the law, which it is safe to say would cause no great amount of evil should practically it were anything else but a Sunday law.
