SOMETIME ago a Cleveland paper reported Wilbur F. Crafts, when organizing a Sunday League in that city, as saying, that a portion of the people of Cleveland had greatly looked for financial aid, but we are going, and we are willing and determined that we will using the right sort of pressure to bear on it.

This harmonizes remarkably well with the different utterances in the article, on another page, entitled, "To Press the Matter in Congress." It has been evident for a considerable time that the promoters of religious laws have been planning to bring, if possible, still greater influences to bear to attain their ends than ever before.

No doubt, too, as the specialty of the situation at this particular time, is the enforcement of a Catholic institution, will they, will there be a sort of a welcome and hearty proffer of assistance from the already established Roman Catholic lobby.

The *Minneapolis Journal* thinks that "in no sense certain classes were possible to this country than the increased prosperity of the Catholic Church, we would be fortunate indeed." If the Roman Church were progressing only as a church, it would be small concern to the country. But it is as a political factor that the Catholic Church is developing most rapidly, hence its power for evil.
