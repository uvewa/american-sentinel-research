They claim the Republic. In regard to the sentiments of the Roman Church, he said they would be "sustained and driven back to the propaganda of American Catholics; who in behalf of themselves and this, our Republic, will ever stand in the forefront of the battle."

They see a union of Church and State. In the rep. of Mr. Crafts, he said: "Your institutions are in accord with the moral spirit of the church and her civilization. In pursuing my mission I find that I am cutting out my own escence of sectarianism, for it must be obvious that I can be a good citizen because I am a good Catholic. The two are one. The Catholic Church and the Republic are made for one another and should walk hand in hand."

This principle is just as clear to day as it was then. At this banquet recently given by Colonel Gibbons, he said: "Church and State can exist peaceably, but not interfere with each other. The Church hierarchy of Rome knew this in the earliest centuries. We would be sorry to see religion and the Church legislated into power."
