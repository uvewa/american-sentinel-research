IF Christianity is not the sole-true and perfect religion, and if all other faiths are not competing false religions, it needs no defense and no parliament.

The last remark is doubtless true: it was indifference, not true Christian character, that made the Parliament of Religions possible.

WE could not have trouble, for the church which is called the body of Christ, admits of one common measure, in that we believe in our one Lord and one savior, that love is the foundation of the gospel.
