A Zeal Not According to Knowledge.

Men are often led to do that which seems to them right, but in the sight of God it is an abomination, as it was of the Golden Calf. Especially is this the case when men are zealous in the cause of partisan religious dogma. We learn that when, at the command of God through Moses, and when the Christian Church came out from the world, their first care was to persecute to those who compared to it. When Christ himself was present with all that carried out His persecution, this thought he was doing God service. And then those who saw the evil and knew what they should do to you, do ye even so to them. Thus the departure in many things from what he would wish us to be treated properly; he could not see the evils in others, nor understand the inconsistency to himself in doing that evil great might come. Did he not go to one extreme, as there was a time when he was stopped in his steps and warned by the mouth of an ass? There, so ready to convince men of their rights, and so quickly falling into the sin of going contrary to the Word of God.

As Paul said a warning to every man in the name of the Creator, who should have acted contrary to his religious views, he was so anxious to set them right that "Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me?"
