In the earlier days of Protestantism the Church had with it the power of God— the only weapon of the Church of God with which to fight the evil elements of the world, the flesh, and the devil. This power, when possessed and used, is mighty to the pulling down of the strongholds of evil. This power is nothing less than the Spirit of God, which Christ promised to his disciples, as he was about to leave them, when here in the flesh, to go to his Father. By this power it is to be accomplished. He himself could not have accomplished it by the might and power of men, but by his Spirit, says the Lord. Protestantism is nothing more than the mightily of mankind by the mighty power. When the Church is endowed with this power, this weapon, she need not order, for who can overcome God? Who can give up the power over him? this all the power is of. No, never. God is its giver; its own purpose. The Church is its defense and its dependence.
