Nothing could more conclusively demonstrate the purely religious character of Sunday laws, as understood and supported by their advocates, than the recent proceedings against Seventh-day Adventists in Tennessee and Maryland. Nothing in those States every man did practically what he pleased on Sunday, under the same police restrictions as surrounded him on other days, and that it had been done for years in the same way. Now then, three Seventh-day Adventist people, pious and peaceable and perfectly law-abiding citizens, it is a matter of history, that "Saturday" is the original "Sabbath," or rest day, of the Bible, and that Sunday is the feast of the pagan sun. Nothing is said of being "established" as a general rest-day or Sabbath in its heavy by Constantius of that further teach, rightly or wrongly is not to be argued here, because it is matter of doctrine, that the acceptance of the pagan substitution of Sunday for the biblical Sabbath by the Christian Churches constituted a "great falling away" from the truth. And for bringing this indictment, and by no means for doing, like all around them, whatever work they chose on Sunday, these Seventh-day Adventists are harried and badgered, and persecuted, rather than

prosecuted, under the forms of the Sunday law, and compelled to pay fines, and in some cases sent to the chain-gang. "And any way by his alleged work?"
