IN Genesis 2:2 we read: "And on the seventh day God ended his work which he had made; and he rested on the seventh day from all his work which he had made. And God blessed the seventh day and sanctified it: because that in it he had rested from all his work which God created and made."

This is the foundation of the weekly Sabbath. It was on the seventh day of the week that God rested, and this day he then to be "sanctified," a character which it has never lost. No word from the Almighty declares it off. Man is not competent to change what God has fixed. Man cannot put into his words lest he reprose thee and deal with him in love.

In Genesis 8:10, if we have a glimpse of this weekly cycle: "Yet is Noah waited yet other seven days to sending out the dove."

It is sometimes stated that God gave no law to mankind until he gave it at Sinai. In the contrary the existence of sin proves the existence of law, for where no law is, there is no transgression. "Sin is the transgression of the law." 1 John 3:4. And where there was no law, "Sin is not." It is told it was corrupt, for all flesh had corrupted his way upon the earth." Gen. 6:12. "His way" much have been made known. Since before they could be held responsible for corrupting it.

In Genesis 18:19, the them of Sodom are spoken of as "sinners exceedingly," so that they have been sinners violated of God's law. This is made plain in Genesis 26:5, where God says unto Isaac of Abraham: "Because that Abraham obeyed my voice, and kept my charge, my commandments, my statutes, and my laws." Abraham was contemporary with the Soledanties. He obeyed the laws which they violated, and became "the father of the faithful." In Exodus 16, when the children of Israel were in the wilderness, before the law was given at Sinai, the law of the Sabbath was in existence and is referred to before the Israelites reached Sinai. In Exodus 16, when the children of Israel came to the wilderness of Sin, which lies between Elim and Sinai, before they had reached Sinai, before the giving of the law at the "mount which burned with fire," and the laws of God were given to the Israelites, the Sabbath law was made a test of their loyalty. "That I may prove them whether they will walk in my law or no." Exodus 16:4. The wonderful presence of God came before Israel, with its miraculous stipulations, and Israel sustained for forty years. The day entered into the land of Canaan and this hosanna-ing but day of rest as it was an object lesson of weekly conformity and duty.

This eliminates all possible errors of dates or seasons or numbering from the days of the week to the time of Christ. There can be no possible error left. To go back to creation and from thence trace for evidence concerning the Sabbath and the first day of the week and man's duty toward God. The Sabbath was made for man, not man for the law, and so divinely was the observance commanded.

In Acts 1:12, "But when the fullness of the time was come, God sent forth his Son, born of a woman, made under the law, to redeem them that were under the law, to redeem those that were under the law, that we might receive the adoption of sons," that he comes down to our nature that he might be with us, up, be partaker of our nature. That is the meaning of this "Son," in Col. 1:16, "For by him were all things created that are in heaven, and that are in earth, visible and invisible, whether they be thrones, or dominions, or principalities or powers; all things were created by him and for him," that he is before all things and by him all things consist. When did he make the Sabbath? Certainly at no subsequent time. Those who have always known its true Sabbath day, for those who have been with the Lord. He it was who "sanctified" it. He still kept it as a Sabbath day, for he was born according to the law, and in Luke 4:16, he declared himself to be "the Lord of the Sabbath." It is a significant and remarkable fact that the Jews had gathered around the Sabbath an enormous mass of ceremonial rubbish, and wound up his teaching by declaring to them that the Son of man is Lord also on the Sabbath days." He justified acts of necessity and mercy that had been done the seventh day in full force, or his only purpose was to show that the Sabbath was—"Julian Calendar," established by Julius Cæsar, from whom it derives its name, was adopted in the month of July. He established his calendar on the basis of a better computation of time than any system which had before obtained. He was the first of the twelve Cæsars. The month of January, just as the month of August follows his example.

It was the decree of Cæsar Augustus that all the world should be "taxed," as given by Luke, that brought Joseph and Mary to Bethlehem, where Jesus was born. Cæsar's estimate of the length of the year is now absolutely stamped upon all the world in its correction by Pope Gregory XIII. Our calendar purely and simply is what Cæsar's was, with the change of style to what is called now new style. The days of the week are the same. To us it is to-day as our daily parish. This day of the weekly cycle is not affected by this change. If the Russian Church has the mass order for the old style, it is observed in all countries of the west of Europe. It would be impossible to satisfy all religious bodies of all sorts. I think that many of the Catholic clergy, if permitted to do so, would rather arrive our normal reports. He would not favor the division of public school funds among the religious denominations.
