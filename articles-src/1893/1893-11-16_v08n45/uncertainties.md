# Uncertainties — American Sentinel, November 16, 1893 (v08n45)

## General Note

This issue presented significant transcription challenges. The scan quality across all 6 pages is poor, with small, dense text that is frequently blurred or difficult to resolve at the character level. Many passages required best-guess readings. The following are the most notable uncertainties:

## Page 1 — Editorial (01)

- **Entire article**: The editorial text by W. H. McKee is extremely difficult to read due to the dense, small typeface and poor scan resolution. Multiple words and phrases throughout are best-guess readings. The column layout on page 1 was also difficult to parse—the editorial appears to flow from the left column to the middle column to the right column, then continues onto page 2.
- **Confidence**: LOW for many individual phrases and sentences throughout this article.

## Pages 1-2 — "Did Papists Establish Religious Liberty in Maryland?" (02)

- The block-quoted text from the American Cyclopædia is difficult to read in places. Names and specific historical details may contain errors.
- **Confidence**: MEDIUM overall, LOW for the quoted historical material.

- **Congressional Record dialogue**: This article contains extensive quotes from the Congressional Record. The speakers' names and their remarks are difficult to read in many places. The flow of the congressional debate is hard to follow due to interruptions and the small typeface.
