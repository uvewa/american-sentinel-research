A CATHOLIC paper complains that the Catholic clergy in Russia still continues with increased ardor on the part of government and their officials. The new Governor of Vilna, called Oroff, has inaugurated his appointment by still more stringent persecutions, "from two to six years."

Despotic governments have always subjected their priests, but they were only in general on the charge that their presence was a menace for the peace of the district. Another point, it is asserted, has been confused in one of the convent prisons, independently, not standing on with his brother priests were taking the oath to the Emperor. "It is the old story," recounts the Catholic contemporary, of the world and the laity. Every pretext is seized that ingenuity can conceive to harass the Catholic priesthood.

All this simply confirms that which we have frequently remarked, namely, that religious intolerance seeks in every country to fix its ugly head, behind substantially the same subterfuges.
