## Opening Session of the Parliament of Religions.

The first session of the World's Parliament of Religion has been held. The Parliament is a reality; unique, and, in all probability, in some sense, the most remarkable and momentous event of the century. Upon the platform, at the opening hour, were gathered representatives of all the important and divergent religions, by men of widely varying opinion, and the whole spectacle was as novel and interesting as it was suggestive.

Just so men stood whilst Cardinal Gibbons impressively opened the first impressive session. The leaders and representatives of the religious congresses have nevertheless extended a pressing invitation to all people to attend. Their programme for the 1893 constitutes what may with perfect propriety be termed "A Religious Declaration of Independence." This document is addressed to the general parliament of religions directly expressed by the government to the whole people of every religion: Russia, Turkey, Greece, Egypt, Syria, Japan, India, Sweden, Norway, Holland, and indeed a many other countries. The nations and religions are included: Christianity in all forms (including Protestants of all denominations): Judaism, Mohammedanism, Hinduism, Buddhism, Parseeism, Jainism, all Oriental religions. Catholicism, the Greek Church, Protestantism in all forms, and the other religions to be stated are considered.

The programme also announces for presentation at the Parliament "the important truths held by all religions: the grounds of union, the ethical unity of different religious systems, history, records, scriptures and manifestations of religion generally, and the leaders of human progress by whom these great religions have been founded or reformed."

## EACH TO OFFER OTHER CREEDS

The third part of the general programme for the sessions of the Parliament, in its more distinctly parliamentary aspect, consists of denominational presentations for the purpose of more fully illustrating the general subjects. These sessions will be held, for the most part, in halls adjacent to that in which the general Parliament meets. Halls for special reasons, already been built. It is the purpose of the Committee of Organization to afford opportunity for the best presentation of all who may desire it. The leaders of those several denominations and their followers and similar national congresses will each be held during the sessions of the Parliament of Religions.

The fourth and final part of the programme of the Parliament will present the work of the congresses of various kindred organizations. It includes: Evangelical Alliance, State and National Sunday, Free Religious Association, various missions, reform, Sunday rest, Salvation, Alliance, Christian Endeavor and others.

We meet on the mountain height of absolute respect and mutual confidence, for all other faiths offer to their devotees. (Cheers.) The very basis of the Parliament is the confidence that the truths of any religion can well endure the tests, and the truest and the best of all; and that they will be thereby shown to the world as worthy of universal acceptance and that as representing the great questions of the immortal life.

Let one other point be clearly stated. While the members of this Parliament meet, first of all, on the common ground of perfect equality, the ecclesiastical or individual limitations of the members are in no way to be compromised. No one shall be expected to make concession of his convictions and special tenets of belief. No rights of the members of this Congress are to be limited by the membership of the Parliament in any degree by its relation to any other. In the second place, we seek in this congress the bond of unity that consists not in uniformity, but which finds in such religions appeal all divisions; to make the best of each other. To seek to discover what things are common to all, and what are good in each other, in the good deeds of the religious life. Whoso is wise, and the Lord make such to agree among themselves upon any matter of faith or worship, or religious life on creation of all mankind, with an earnest

desire to be useful to each other and to all others whom they can reach with their influence and progress over the world, through the spirit of brotherhood. This religious sentiment, filling the air with its exquisite perfume. This cry of the heart for the unseen, this yearning of God in the hearts of men. He and down and far over and through all that the world is filled by, God the hearts of those who worship God and finds in all religions the very presence of God. It is the brotherhood of religions.

It is quite possible that this is the beginning of the last era in the world's history of evil, in which it is "heaped to the full" and in which the Lord arises against all who do evil, and who profess it against all who do not profess it, and he destroys with the brightness of religion its contradistinction to the progress of spiritual truth and religion. In the words of the speaker, "unite all religions against irreligion."

*Chicago, Sept. 17.*
