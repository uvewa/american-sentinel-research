It is noticeable that while the interest tended to be in the Interest of Sunday observance a form in the "Sunday Sabbath" appears, but instead the word "Sabbath," which, entirely defunct applicable to Sunday by the advocates of the day. And this is addressed to ministers who are supposed to know "the Scriptures." What a strange discrepancy for intelligent men to make! From the *Herald's* own *Wis.*Daily: Who is deceived by this? the advocates or the People?

The fact that the historic legislative fast-day of Massachusetts has changed from a religious institution to one of innocent games and revelry, has led to an attempt on the part of the present governor to set apart the day by proclamation without giving it a religious repeal created not a little discussion and the dragging of the State, an anti-climax elimination was brought to bear upon the legislature to retain the statute recognizing it as a religious holiday. In deference to this the legislative recognition of fast-day, the present governor, accordingly, has issued a proclamation in words which any good and pure profitable mind to be couched in more acceptable form. We publish it for our readers.
