EDITOR SENTINEL:—For some months past the writer has been a casual, and lately a constant, reader of the AMERICAN SENTINEL, in connection with general religious and legal, and literary, secular and biblical, ancient and modern.

Observing the present trend of legislative enactments and the bold, swelling difference with which the American people their rights to be invaded, the demand for religious legislation which surrounds for demands of creatance and morality, virtually to the very evident subversion of the very principles for which people have struggled and died for their freedom to worship God"—the position taken by the SENTINEL is timely and sound, and it brave and timely.
