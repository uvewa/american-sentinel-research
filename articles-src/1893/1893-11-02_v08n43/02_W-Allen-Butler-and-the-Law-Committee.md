The same man is pointed out as a lawyer of the greatest legal acumen and highest prestige in the ranks of those who seek and work for religious laws and legislation. That is to with these that they do this is shown by published documents from his own pen. The country, and we realize the inconsistencies involved in the constitutional amendment which the league proposes, the question submitted in this letter, and the contradictory positions on matters of religious law and legislation, taken by the promoters and officers of this organization, must require something more than mere protestation before they can believe that this league is anything other than another American Protective Association in disguise.

But the American Protective Association cannot or need not appeal to any real equality to comprehend the principles involved, for the reason that its purposes are palpable on the face of them. We admit the desirable of the two, for it leaves no doubt as to its purpose in its country, and admits that it does not love its enemy.

In this series of questions which the league has sent out it commits and confesses itself. The link of consistency between the constitutional amendment and the question in this letter, and the anomalous questions, show conclusively that it is not followed out in principle even if its use may accepte understandings of them as they actually apply to each other.

The humiliating truth seems to be that the organization of the American Institutions is nothing more than a polished political machine secretly seeking to aggrandize itself by antagonistic religious purposes. It is, in fact, but little exhibition of the same force from the identical standpoint.
