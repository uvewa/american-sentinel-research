Results of "Christian" Sunday Laws.

(Continuation of list of items in from the Christian Endeavor resolutions and church actions across the country, continually increasing in frequency; space will not admit the complete record.)

—The city council of Napa, pass an ordinance and a certain ordinance closing the saloons of that city on Sunday.

—The Trade Association of Philadelphia petitions the Governor for strict enforcement of a proposal to close drug stores on Sabbath unless there be a registered pharmacist on hand.

—The Mayor of Montgomery, Mobile and at the other large cities of the State of Alabama, arrest "Jews' bakers" on Sunday.

—The Lord's Day Observance Association has petitioned the British and Colonial legislatures to define the class of voters at elections, otherwise held on Sundays; to provide that such elections shall hereafter be held on week days. Christianity, when held, the usual enfanglements and intermixture with the State.

—The Grocers' Protective Association and retail butchers of Sioux City, Iowa, have made a combined movement to have all their places of business closed on the Sabbath.

September 5 was the first day a butcher's Sunday law was in force there.

—A combination of sentiment, with two or three elements of the market trade was made in the interest of Sunday rest, and was urged by their followers to secure certain needed rest of the people.

The Grocers' Protective Association of Kansas City, finding Sunday closing of beneficial merit and to promote the proper observance of Sunday, and to secure the passing of laws enforcing Robinson's Strict Sunday law.— Four butchers of Knoxville, Tenn., were arrested for violation of the law farther of that city for Sunday selling.

—The grocers and butchers of Des Moines, Ia., also form a Union Sabbath Committee on similar lines.

—A citizen of Kansas City writes "we do not know whether the laws protect them or persecute them for Sunday trading; that Sunday sales of provisions are spent to all classes brought to him with proper energy. No citizen should object."

—"Pearl of Days" column in the *Mail and Express*.
