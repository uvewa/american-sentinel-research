CHEAP EXCURSIONS AND CONVENTIONS.

What the West Shore Railroad Has Done.

THE DAYS ON WHICH THE SPECIAL WILL RUN.

West Shore Railroad has been operating special trains for World's Fair visitors, leaving points along the service, high class every way, coaches, parlor cars, Pullman sleeping cars, dining cars available. West Shore trains both continental service for the Fair. West Shore both comfortable and expeditious.

The trains stop at all principal stations, giving local travelers easy access to the trains from their own modern stations, and tickets are good on all regular trains. The West Shore road will continue the service while the Fair lasts. It will be impossible to find a better or more comfortable way to make the trip, and the comfort of the excursionists has been well looked after. Ask your local ticket agent, or address the West Shore offices, regional, regard well long before the dates of excursion, so that sleeping car space can be held.

To readers, as follows: Wednesday, July 26 is the next date.

For further information in regard to the above, the West Shore R.R. published special circulars that can be had for the asking by addressing H. B. Jagoe, General Eastern Passenger Agent, 363 Broadway, New York.
