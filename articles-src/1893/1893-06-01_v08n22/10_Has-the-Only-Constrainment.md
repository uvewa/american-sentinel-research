HAS the only constrainment for the secular courts decided in the matter of the Sunday opening of the World's Fair, in its Harrisonian right to use public funds to bribe people to observe Sunday? If the Federal Government has such power my might as not secure the observance of any religious rite or institution, in the same manner? But to the SENTINEL the proposition seems as clear as the noonday sun that Congress has no power to do by indirection that which it is by the Constitution forbidden to do directly.

The Sign of the Times, an Adventist paper published in California, has this to say about its misstatement in Michigan: "The editor of the Christian Sabbath Watch, published at Detroit, Mich., warns the State has a just law prohibiting Seventh-day Adventist observers from pursuing their occupations. Many will look upon such a purpose as an aim, but the wiser ones will see in it more radical measures than the find place in the statute books of nearly every State in the Union. Before many years there will be an agitation to fill this bill in one of spirit now, and it will soon settle down."
