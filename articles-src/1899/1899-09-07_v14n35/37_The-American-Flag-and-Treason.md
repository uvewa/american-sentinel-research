THE mere presence of the American flag floating over a body of armed men, does not consecrate the cause in which those men may be enlisted. If the sultan of Turkey should come with a band of assassins under that flag, resistance to him would not be treason to the American Government. If a band of robbers in this country should come flying the American flag and attack the citizens of a town, no principle of loyalty to the United States would oblige those citizens to submit.
