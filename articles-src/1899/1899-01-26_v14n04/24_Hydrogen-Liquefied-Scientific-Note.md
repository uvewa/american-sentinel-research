THE lightest and most volatile of all substances—hydrogen—has been forced at the dictate of modern science to assume liquid form, at a temperature the lowest ever measured by man. The air in a closed tube dipped into the liquid is immediately frozen, securing a more perfect vacuum than is possible by any other means.
