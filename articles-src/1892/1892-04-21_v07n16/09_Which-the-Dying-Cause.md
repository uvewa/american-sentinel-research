# Which the Dying Cause?

Mr. F. W. Ware, Secretary of the American Sabbath Union of the State of Michigan, in his paper for February and March, says:â€”

> The Seventh-day Adventist papers are guilty of serious injustice toward the friends of the Sabbath by saying that those who are laboring to secure proper legislative safeguards for the Sabbath, from ordinary labor and noisy sports are doing this from religious motives. They are probably not more right than are those who deny their course in this matter.

One of two things must be true: this religious organization, Union, in its efforts on behalf of Sunday, either is or is not trying to make men religious by law, or trying to make men religious by law. If is all, for this is its sole work, aim, and purpose. If the latter, it makes men religious by law. If it, a religious organization, is not trying to make men religious, it would cease to turn its attention to its legitimate and understood line of work. There is little question, however, as to what it is trying to do. The positions taken at the questionnaire feature in the several meetings, prove it conclusively. It may be said to compel further that it must be a poor and dying Sabbath that is compelled to resort to law for protection.
