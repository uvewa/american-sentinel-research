In its issue of May 12, the Congregationalist advocates what it calls "a cautious policy" in regard to Sunday laws. "But a cautious policy," it observes, "does not mean a policy of inaction." The Congregationalist continues:â€”

The movement for Sabbath reform, like that for temperance reform, has suffered from impatient and ill-considered efforts to realize at once what, as all history shows, must be the fruit of years and perhaps decades of earnest, ceaseless labor.

The suggestion is significant in view of the herculean efforts being put forth to get Congress to commit the Nation in some way in favor of Sunday.
