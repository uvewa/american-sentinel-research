It has been said, and truthfully, that if this country were a Christian, it would then be a "Nation of Christians;" not a "Nation of Christians." Christianity is an individual thing; not an institutional one. And properly the term "Christian nation" can not, with propriety, be applied to any country even; though every individual were perfect in character. And of the people can appropriately be called a nation composed wholly of Christian individuals, how much less should it be applied to a nation in which so compared, most of the people are not Christians?
