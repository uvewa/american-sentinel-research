After them were the Quakers. And who did not go were forcibly taken to the church. The Baptists and Quakers did not believe in the divine sanction of the ceremonies of the established religion. They therefore refused to comply. Their refusal, of course, was construed as; as non-conformity was a violation of the law, and they were fined; but they again refused to pay or to comply with the required ceremonies. They were then fined still more; but again they refused. They were then banished, and yet they refused; and the Quakers even refused to be banished. Then they were hanged; and not these with the condition that they could now comply with the required provisions; but that they would not be permitted, having to go from there on their refusal.
