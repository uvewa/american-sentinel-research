THE National meeting of the American Sabbath Union held under the auspices of the local branch at Omaha on a recent occasion was an assembly of mark. Any one would have been attracted to see the dignified ecclesiastical and civic dignity which characterized the proceedings of the occasion. Sent on the platform, three bishops, nine doctors of divinity, Judge Lawrence, of Ohio, and Elliot F. Shepard. Bishop Hare presided. New York was represented by Rev. Manly Hard, of New York, led the devotions. Nearly 400 clergymen were present. The speakers were limited to ten minutes each, and those who spoke were: Bishop Hare, Rev. Dr. Burrows, of San Francisco; W. F. Crafts, Rev. G. S. Robinson, of Episcopacy; Holby Warner, Judge Lawrence, Rev. Dr. Burrows, of San Francisco; Rev. Dr. Corrigan, of Canada; Rev. G. S. Robinson, Bishop Fowler, and Rev. W. H. Hamilton.

Mr. Crafts gave the history and standing of the Sunday observance movement. He said the American Sabbath Union, had nineteen secretaries who devoted all their time to the work of the Union and fifty-two others who gave a portion of their time. When he reviewed what had been done to secure the Sunday closing of the World's Fair he was warmly applauded. In closing Mr. Crafts said: "the Sabbath as imperialists commanded, from the maintenance of salvation in the preservation of manna in the wilderness to the last word that the world heard of the formal Sunday observance upon those premises.
