THE *Jewish Messenger* flatters itself that the platform of the Society of Christian Endeavor is "a plan for the unification of the human race," and then advances—

Here is an opportunity for the Society of Christians to show some justice by preventing the violation of rights of nearly one-half a dozen of new converts. Let them now be foremost in defending the rights of the Jews. Let us hear them pass some resolutions against the persecution of those in their midst who observe Saturday and in whose devotion the Christ they worship had part.

The flaw, in commenting upon this, and other favorable expressions in reference to the great Christian Endeavor convention's Jewish papers, finds in it all a pleasing "evidence of the great fact that in this country, the believer in every creed, not only matches the rights of all creeds, but can live together in peace with his neighbors."
