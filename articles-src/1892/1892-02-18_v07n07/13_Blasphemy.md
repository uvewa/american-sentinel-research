Responses to a recent infidel lecture in Brooklyn, Dr. Talmage said:—

Can any man be a thing so blasphemous, for there is so there is no law against it? These are tremendous responsibilities and the land is in that last phase between the judges of our courts and through them the law statutes of the State of New York, neither was its charge as blasphemy. Let the law against this as being to stand from the statutes the book or in the Legislature. It would have been the greatest crime do Brooklyn had ever witness it but last Sabbath my friend Patrick Campbell, the chief of the Police. It did believe in Christ—in Jesus Christ our children, and put his hand on the shoulder to protect them, it is one of the noblest statutes of the common law. In the name of the State of New York, and in the name of the People of this, this infamy must stop—must stop have been ready also for.

Yes, there is such a thing as blasphemy, and the time was when Dr. Talmage, by his life and preached and as he now preaches, would have been put to death for blasphemy.

“Blasphemy,” says Bouvier, “is to attribute to God that which is contrary to his nature, and does not belong to him, and to deny what does.” It follows that every man who has as a direct consequence of God from that which his neighbor has, blasphemies, in the estimation of that neighbor, if he gives expression to that conception. Thus, in the eyes of the Trinitarians, the Unitarians blasphemes

when he denies the divinity of Christ and the personality of the Holy Spirit. Civil laws against blasphemy are dangerous things.
