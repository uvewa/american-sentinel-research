———

The Fortworth Ward Improvement Association, of Newark, New Jersey, under the head of detective work, has resolved, among other things, to enforce the Sunday laws of Newark. The Newark *Times* criticizes it as an assumption of the executive and judicial functions of the city.

———

In the New York Legislature, Senator Hasner has introduced a joint resolution urging the Representatives of the Nation in Congress to use their influence with the World’s Fair Commissioners not to open the Fair on Sunday; and that saying that they favor it as common to freedom of evil and emergency, except to be compelled with a resolution that the Fair be open to visitors and suspended as an Exposition on the first day.

———

The *Evening Dispatch,* of Monday, February 1, records that at the Sunday morning hearing, February 1, before the police judge, there were 27 cases. Names: Magistrate Storey, twenty cases; Magistrate Brady, six cases; Magistrate Lodge, one case; Magistrate Leslie, one case. The Grand Order Lodge to secure an indictment of those magistrates for punishing their constituents on Sunday; on the plea that this hearing of cases on Sunday is a violation of the Sunday law.

A layout trade and Sunday labor Prohibitionist Association of Minneapolis says that the Prohibition Party is not a temperance society, but a political party, and that among other things it proposes to suppress the liquor traffic; and that one way by which to secure the “civil” and rest of cure is by the party, is to stop altogether disloyal Sabbath reform.— A more direct route to make the matter prominent. And in the other way to end the Exposition.

The bullshaft plank of the Prohibition Party is good for at least that Sunday legislation being a good thing if it is made permanent. “This is a frank statement of the alliance of the Prohibition Party with the religious legislatures.”

In regard to the educational situation, bill, and the Emperor says “conducts the Emperor’s aide desire to recognize education in Prussia on a denomination basis.” The bill, as described in the message of the Prussian State; that is to say, as controlling the Catholic and Protestant religions. ———

The Wisconsin Sunday Rest Association held its meeting at Madison, Wisconsin, recognized as a branch of the American Sabbath Union, and adopted lengthy resolutions in regard to the Sunday closing of the World’s Fair; and Sunday observance in general. This Association at its inception, attempted to confine itself only to “the civil” side of the question, and proves the possibility of the enforcement of a civil Sunday as a day of rest that the secular and religious should be observed on the same day. The “civil” the only an impossible, but only the name covers up virtually all the processes of legal religion.
