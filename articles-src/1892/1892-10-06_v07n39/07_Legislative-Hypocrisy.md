# Legislative Hypocrisy

The action of the Democratic Congress in voting to close the World's Fair on Sunday has had the effect of causing the multitudes of the country to organize themselves into a voting party. To close the World's Fair gates is to make the enjoyment of the country on the only day when laboring people can attend a mere luxury. The effect is well illustrated by a fact, as a Paul at Rome is represented as carrying a citizen's, and the papers announce that in front of the gates to the Exposition, "a saloon has been opened, and the pious people go one way toward the gate and the impious crowd go the other way toward the saloon, "Pack gives the master at the correct rate. "An unholy alliance," is what the Church and the rum shop ally themselves to by divide the caution of visitors to Chicago. The result will be that while the Church may catch five hundred of those who would otherwise attend the Fair, the saloon will absorb five thousand. In the interests of sobriety the Church should be willing to let the saloon be supplanted by a place where public morals might be tempted.

The hypocrisy of the members of Congress so much the worse is this fact that a large majority of the individual legislators who voted for the closing of the Fair have sufficient respect for Sunday to observe it themselves, and they are as likely to go with one crowd as the other.
