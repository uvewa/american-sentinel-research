Petitions against Congressional Legislation.

ON May 1 petitions were presented in the House of Representatives, praying Congress not to enact the United States Government to an establishment of religion and the State by the passage of any legislation closing the World's Columbian Exposition on Sunday, or in any other way commanding the Government to a recovery of religious legislation.

Petitions were presented from citizens of Illinois, Iowa, Galion, Ohio, Oregon, Virginias, Wisconsin, Pennsylvania, Ohio, and other places, from Booneration, Ill., Mercer County, Ill., Ogdensburg, N. Y., Moline, Ill., Vineland, N. J., Pottstown, County, Ark., Florence, Colo., New Hampshire County, Conn., Wis., Franklin County, Ohio, Huntington County, N. Y., Oswego County, N. Y., Hackensack, Colo., Lake County, Colo., Grand Detour, N. Y., Dixon County, Ill., other counties and Dakota County, Mich., Mora County, Ohio, Ingham County, Ala., Isabella County, Mich., Gratiot County, Colo., Arapahoe County, Colo., Baltimore Co., Md., Langlade, Wis., Buffalo, N. Y., New Castle County, Del., and Cattaraugus County as many places.

Not stated in the *Congressional Record*, from what individuals these petitions came, or were offered simply as from citizens, others from organizations, and others still from towns.

This and a continuation of this, should be good and sufficient evidence to the World's Fair Commission of the Senate of Illinois to which the petitions are referred, that their constituents are awakening to the fact that the advocating of a union of religion and the State, are striving to and then into, decrease legislative grafts, in the legislation session. Precedent makes it both compelling!
