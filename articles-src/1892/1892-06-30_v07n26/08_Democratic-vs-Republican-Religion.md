# Democratic vs. Republican Religion.

THAT exemplar of the perfection of political religions, and which, according true to its political instinct had, no criticism or analysis, the Republican National Convention, when, in session at Minneapolis, they ignored all the prayers and longings for the great and good Presbyterian churches, had not the suggestion of the English language are scarcely sufficient to furnish inventive to heap up invective upon the Democratic Convention; and the delegates at Chicago because Democratic politicians were not able to agree in their politics. But this is not partisan in politics, and to wears no party bias or party blindness from the nearest fact, that according to the great newspaper authorities themselves, party politics are religious and Democratic politics are irreligious. This is the another evidence that when the *Mail and Express* has said, as it so frequently has, "this is a Christian Nation" it has distinctly and intentionally intended as interesting to say that religion has no place in the Republican. If Republican politics being the Christian religion and the American Sabbath Union being the exponent of that religion, which to the *Mail and Express* alike are related as one for Republicanism, it naturally participates in its own creed, that that bibliotheca pertains as to the Sabbath being made for man, and, applying it to the political Sabbath, the Republican Sabbath or the Republican Party and not the Republican Party and not the Republican party should clearly Republicize in character, the Democratic Party has no right to sit in it and to it, and any one of the republican politicians who are in a Sunday observance are detected.
