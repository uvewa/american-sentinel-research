# Limitations to Majority Rule.

THE September number of the *Tideus Tegn*, Copenhagen, has an article on "State Authority" which though general in character, contains principles that, if reference to the United States, or even to its application in Europe, deserves the serious attention of American Christians.

> It indicates democracy of the political digma that the majority has an absolute right to rule the minority. This right has been formerly attached, in the minds of men, to royal rulers; but when the thought been transferred to the law, the question arises, Is the minority obliged, is the minority obliged to obey the majority? If so, where is the limit? And have not they would compel one to obey another, because the two was greater than the one and the former? If the majority rule is the right, then every majority, small or great, is right, a man becomes a slave and not a citizen to man.

> Is no man American? he is no American in his constitution; he is an alien that every government; but is the light of the truth of God he has the absolute truth of American liberty.

> But that the majority has the absolute truth of life to rule the minority.
