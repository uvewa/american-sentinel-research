# Union or Not?

IN a lecture not long since, Rev. Joseph Cook said that no intelligent person now believes in the "union of Church and State." Mr. Cook is mistaken. Intelligent people seem to have learned enough about religious liberty to know that Church and State must be kept separate; but very many demonstrate the real meaning of that and religion.

Mr. Cook went on to say that Sunday ought to be kept more holy, and he wanted a lot more people to keep it holy; others have denied the main. Now, if three people understand his meaning, will they join the "Christian State"? Since they have the Bible, containing precepts that have force in the civil government, what scriptural idea of this is there? Where does the union of Church and State, the union of it? of God and of earthly governments and have to do with God's State and its relation.

Jesus (Matt. 22: 21): "Render, therefore unto Caesar the things which are Caesar's, and unto God the things that are God's." Christ, and at the last and the earliest ancient empire of Rome, was the representative of all earthly government.
