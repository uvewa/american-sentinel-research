# Conflicting Opinions.

THE following letters, both from ministers of South Dakota, in regard to Protestant Christianity religion, were casually received by the International Tract Society during one week. They clearly express the opinions of such as to the correspondence, manifested by this specific movement to the correspondence, and publish a paper like THE SENTINEL:

I find THE SENTINEL, with special interest, and I agree with it that the Church and State are two separate institutions, and that it is not right to constrain any individual from what he considers right and his conscience requires. But I do not believe it has for its object the keeping of Sunday or not.
