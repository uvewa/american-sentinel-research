# A Timely Satire.

THE *Christian Endeavor,* of Chicago, last week published the following:

The Chicago City Council, with its customary disregard for the wishes of the church people, last week failed to declare St. Patrick's Day a legal holiday. This act is a base violation of the religious rights of our Catholic fellow-citizens. There is no excuse for such action; on the 4th instant, Dr. F. S. Henson presented a petition from Baptist churches, asking the City Council to declare the day a holiday; and the then mentioned by our City Council is due to be found in the failure to recognize the rights of its best fellow-citizens, and that William Penn founded the Colony of Pennsylvania for the benefit of the Baptists, that Roger Williams, and John Wesley is deference to in the founding of Rhode Island, founded the Presbyterians, and Martin Luther in deference to the Lutherans. When these facts are properly understood and their proper influence is an influential factor at the polls, then the representatives of the people will learn that such cavalier disregard, the members of the City Council of citizens in whose hand the city offices shall be placed in the future. We call upon the religious calendar to serve the purpose. The satire of this is better than many an argument.
