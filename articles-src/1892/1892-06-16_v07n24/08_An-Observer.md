# An Observer.

Instead of the printers appealing to Mr. Crafts for protection, he patronizes. Who can see through the flimsy drapery of the thing? We assure the printers of the country that the so-called interests of this pious to secure his divested union of Church and State is to be carefully watched, as most printers are ignorant of these things.

His law reformers read some history, and know how "the encounters of the power of bigoted classes to fasten religious opinions on others by force and fraud, to justify past losses and inconvenience, and never, too, that the darkest crimes committed in the past, of history were perpetrated by the very class of individuals who boasted they had secured power.

Mr. Crafts is not a bad man, deliberately, but his mistaken zeal has made him foolish.
