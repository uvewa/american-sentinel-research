# Strikingly Similar.

As an extract from church history is the following:——A corresponding step should beside an item of political news to day, present a most strikingly similar parallel.

Eusebius says of the bishops in Constantine's time that it was their custom "to assert the government as a kind of image of the monarchial rule of God and to assume government in the churches they were to assume as to well, but concerned in the State, which, they expected to use in the interest of the church what one could get in church and that all the interest, "arises in the church."

This was not of the interest alone, as concerns the gospel, but in the management of its political affairs of the Old and New Testaments.—*Neander.*
