THE people of this quiet neighborhood were once noted for their good morals and attention to their duties. That is, until the Adventists came in and commenced disturbing all the people with their peculiar doctrine.

The Adventists desire to avoid if possible any collision with the authorities, and will not surrender their conscience for the dictation of any human authority.

The Adventists believe that the fourth commandment requires that they rest from work the seventh day of the week and not other days; and that it is a sin to work on the true Sabbath.” They feel that they have been driven to habitation solely on secular labor and business on any day of the week except the seventh, and since the arrest of several of their number for “Sunday labor,” last spring, they have neither worked on Sunday nor on their Sabbath rest.
