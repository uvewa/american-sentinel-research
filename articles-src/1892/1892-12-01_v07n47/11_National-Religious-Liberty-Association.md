# National Religious Liberty Association.

KAVANAUGH, KY., Nov. 18---The Mason County grand jury has finished its work, and brought indictments against nearly all the barber shops, cigar stores and other places of business that were open on Sunday.

---

THOSE who have back numbers of THE AMERICAN SENTINEL, or copies of the *Young People's Worker*, or *Pacific Health Journal*, or *Little Friend*, can find a market for them. Let us refer any orders to us by making them. The publications can be sent postpaid to J. A. Dortch, at the Tract and Missionary Society and their rooms, Seattle, Washington. The people of these parts for the truth and are asking for it.

THE pastors of the different churches of the city of Elgin, Illinois, have organized an auxiliary of the American Sabbath Union. One of its first official duties will be to petition the Legislature to make the reading of the Bible a daily exercise in the public schools.

THE Cincinnati *Post* of November 18 has the following:---

Constable George Berry and Garret Havens were arrested in the jail here for labor law for picking potatoes on Sunday. They are poor farmers. They were arrested by Constable Ellis, charged with husking corn on Sunday.

---

THIS Chicago *Herald* produces a strong editorial in favor of Sunday opening. Reverend H. W. Thomas delivered a sermon at McVicker's Theater, Englewood, on the afternoon of Sunday, Nov. 6, in favor of Sunday opening of the Fair.

---

REV. A. T. Jones is among the most ardent of those who are working against the Sunday enactment that the Columbian Exposition be closed on the measure on the Sabbath question. He has been travelling extensively, delivering addresses on the measure on the Sabbath question. He traveled his subject from the negative side, or from the standpoint of freedom for the conscience that the Fair should be open Sundays.

---

A captain in Sierra Leone, says in a private letter in which he acknowledged the receipt of tracts and other gentlemen waited on the governor with a petition asking him to promulgate "the Lord's day," and more especially to prohibit van and boat traffic on that day, either to go to or load their cargoes. What would be the result of our petition. It was of no avail. He asked us to not ask to tell us the time of its handling. In the Far Africa as in the home land, the idea of the religious establishment of power. But however, when it was taken up by Congress the idea was no new thing. The Christian people had been looking for an opportunity for some time, they had been looking for an opportunity for gaining the Nation's recognition and support of their religion.

---

Resolved, That we do hereby petition Congress to repeal the Sunday closing provision of the World's Fair appropriation bill, and to leave the Exposition management free to open on Sunday.

Mr. Crafts and his fellow craftsmen protest, they should find no more words from the "Sabbath." The Sunday closing of the Fair before all of any kind for the World's Fair except the management of the Fair. The movement should be closed on Sunday, so that the working classes could visit it on that day.

---

The *Chronicle* of Bath, England, on November 8, contained a column and a half report of an important meeting held in that city in defense of church establishment. The most of the space was given to an account of the Bath branch of the Lord's Day Observance Society.
