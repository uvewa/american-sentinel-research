# Would Our Forefathers Blush?

THE following dialogue is said to have taken place between an artist and a Catholic priest after the former had painted the pictures of Paul and Peter on the walls of a church:---

Friend---And Patrick, why do you put so much red paint on the pictures of St. Paul and St. Peter?

---And I have continually read the sentiments of Wesley and others with their discoveries of Bible truth. In the pictures as they should look they have thought, if the pictures of the apostles could come to life they would take a good deal of red paint on their portraits.

Let those who think the language too strong, those who, as the present day, are boycotting, oppressing and imprisoning, like foxes, devout and God-fearing men, guilty, with the longest voiced and gorgeously, with the banquet voiced in the following words, put the test to their own speech their statements how they met them!

No man who has the true principles of democracy such as these. The sacrifice of Christ was a voluntary sacrifice; so the religious service must be a voluntary service. "Whosoever will, let him come." Christianity was ever too slow to meet the minds of modern legislators, and so they helped revive it by law. "Whosoever won't, compel him by law."

Who sought the aid of civil law in the matter of religion? Not the genuine apostles? Say, verily. They were the parties who subverted the genuine demands of law. The men who enforced religious notions, and who infringed upon the religious liberty of the people. Not the genuine Jews, who loved tradition more than the truth, and the chief priests. Not the genuine apostles. They had departed from the truth of God, and knew of no other way to carry out their ends than by appeal to the Government.
