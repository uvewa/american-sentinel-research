Toronto, Ontario, has enjoyed for some years the perfect observance of all forms of business on Sunday. The street cars are stopped and only wares of commerce and Industry consume to move, is to be their interest. But more lately even this equilibrium has been upset by the school advocates of Sunday laws, who coming in regard to the general closing of Sunday, the laboring man should be least of them in the spirit. A short time since the Mayor of the city called on the people to vote, on whether or not they should have the street cars run on Sunday. As the following shows Sunday street car laws are up in arms about it, and seem to think that the Mayor has done a very wicked thing in even thinking to submit this question to the people, or let any of them vote at all.

He is right, the people should have the power to decide the great moral questions, but the Mayor proposed that they should vote on it. They do not intend it as an invitation to debate whether we shall keep Sunday or not; it is a vote. Many people will vote for the Sunday street car, not as people insulting alike to God and man.
