# The "Civil Sabbath" vs. Religion.

IT is easy to see that the law, by which the Government undertakes to enforce religion, by placing the human authority to give place. The Sunday closing of the Fair, of Sunday, since compulsory Sunday idle time, instead of voluntary rest, even among Christians, makes the divine institution appear oppressive; and the abuse of things destroys conscience, degrades law and increases crime.

When men are at leisure at the same time, and by compulsion instead of inclination, the temptation to holidays is greatly increased. If the gates of the Fair are closed on Sundays, the saloon gates will open wider. Irreligion men will drift to the saloon, and have more time to spend there.

The demands of business and the allurements of pleasure do not keep the church attendance; no much as does lack of sympathy for the church's real aim, and for religion everyday. Well may Sunday rest advocates say for every Sunday rest, or any other day, there must be religion in the hearts of the people.

1. Cease to depend on civil law, and turn the whole current of discussion into channels of morality and religious effort.

2. Separate all legislation against liquor selling on Sunday from all attempts to class Sunday above any other holiday, or any other forms of business, and to ban from the Sunday statute all mention of effects upon churches.

3. Sunday saloon laws, and all laws restricting other businesses on Sunday as a general category furnishes the saloon with a ready argument, and so allies the church with deadly effect. The effort to close the Fair is at this moment the best argument ever against the full prohibition of the saloon. The saloon, the gambling house and the saloon is always in order.
