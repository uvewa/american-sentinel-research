## "American State Papers"

WE have really entered on the downward grade of restricting and curtailing religious liberty literature. We earnestly implore every reader of the SENTINEL to take a close, earnest, and thoughtful interest in this. It must be apparent to all that there can be nothing more important at this time. We want to circulate our position on this Sunday legislation question, and as widely as possible. And as the people learn that our knowledge of the prophecy is really what has been urged in this line, and as they see that our positions on the law, and the persecutions that are to follow are absolutely correct, many will accept the truth, and be saved. Hence the great importance of sowing the seed now, that the great harvest may follow.

From this vantage in this stand to be well informed on the subject. And if we will all become interested in reading and in their understanding of this very important theme. Every friend of this cause has a certain duty of laborers in the field to read this literature; and then impress upon all with whom they come in contact the importance of reading and studying it. Then as they enter the field in chapter in the result of the work they will feel that they are not afraid to meet a proposition and refute it; and their opponents will also feel the importance of the work more, as they understand more perfectly.

"American State Papers" is one of the best books to give a correct idea of the principles of religious liberty as enunciated by the wisest of the founders of our Government, that we know of. It should be in every home in the land. All citizens should read it, and circulate it as widely as possible. It is a book that will be of inestimable value to put in the hands of editors, lawyers, and men of that class. But while it is good for them, it is none the less valuable for all classes.
