## Four Improved Adventists.

Two four improvised Adventists of using Henry O. Jail, at Paris, Tenn., found themselves in the charges in a just fate. The sheriff who was responsible for their after keeping realized that they were not criminals, and consequently granted them, while in confinement, such privileges as lay within his power. When three of them were sent on the road to work, with either reasons of the prison, the sheriff did not permit them to be chained with the gangs, and unlike or in partial cases of the rest, complained at toil on the Sabbath.

That they did not suffer all the indignities and discomforts of the ordinary criminal, that they were not chained together with the gang to work on the public road; that there was an attempt to compel them, by flogging or starving, to work on the Sabbath, is due to the plain, manly, and individual sense of right and justice of the sheriff of Henry County. It is worthy to be noted that this man had his direction on the side of justice, but it is also well to remember that another sheriff may feel that he does God service by enforcing his full authority over those who are his prisoners for conscience' sake.
