# An Injustice.

[From the *Christian Patriot*, Morristown, Tenn.]

The Sunday laws are unjust at first on general principles. They have two objects: first to assist the church with its weakening grasp upon the people, by enforcing the observance of rest; and, secondly, to prevent the disturbance and annoyance of those who rest on Sunday. Even infidels should concur in those laws which protect the notions of health and intelligence, and that it is just that those who will to worship should be protected from annoyance.

But this law has been used to punish a Seventh-day Adventist. We regret his errors; but if he means not disturb any, or him of them persecution will not be. Is it harmer? He rested on Saturday, a day pronounced on Sunday. He was arrested, tried under the statute. He paid his costs, and refused to pay the fine, preferring to stand the common law. He appealed. It has gone through the State courts and all have affirmed the lower judgment. The United States Circuit Court. It is to be appealed to the Supreme Court. We hope

Mr. King will gain his case. For, first, he has complied substantially with the object of the Sunday laws. He and his family household rest one day in the week, and his ploughing at a distance from any church did not annoy any worshiper.
