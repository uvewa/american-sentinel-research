# Sunday-Law News.

IN New Braunfels, Texas, they are having a brisk agitation of the Sunday law question. The New Braunfels *Herald* takes the proper position in opposition to Sunday laws, that it is not a proper sphere for the exercise of civil authority; and at the same time denies that there is any authority from God for abstaining from labor on Sunday, commending the *Herald* says:

> This is a free country, and at least ought to be, as far as the exercise of individual liberty, provided it does not encroach upon the rights of others. It is a matter of religion, and would come under the pervading principle of the separation of Church and State. I shall worship God, or whether I shall worship Him at all, or whether I go to church on Sunday, or Monday, or Saturday, or any other day. I approved Church and State, and provided that all men should be treated as the equals of other men, and find in his or her own way, and a Sunday law is in violation of those principles. And an attempt to carry religion into politics, or politics into religion, and for that reason ought to be demolished of old.

But the Sunday laws exist. The contition has begun. Why has this not been found out before?
